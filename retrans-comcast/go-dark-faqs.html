Your Local Station on Comcast

Q: How can Comcast just cut off access to a local broadcast station when local broadcast is so important for communities?

A: Unfortunately, Comcast has refused to reach a fair, market-based agreement to keep your access to local programming. We are committed to reaching fair deals with every pay-TV provider because that's how we are able to deliver the absolute best local news and weather, sports and entertainment, and to be there when our neighbors need us.

We hope Comcast appreciates how important access to our station is and preserves your access by coming to a fair and market-based agreement.

Q: How can I watch my favorite sports, news and entertainment programs?

A: You have choices. Our station is available on other service providers in our community. Visit the “Switch Now” section of this website to learn more.

Q: Why is Comcast refusing to reach an agreement to carry your station?

A: We cannot speak for Comcast. But here is what you should know: We are committed to reaching fair, market-based agreements with all the video service providers in our area. We've successfully reached hundreds of multi-year deals with distributors across the country over the past several years, including other major cable companies like Verizon, Spectrum and others. Unfortunately, Comcast has refused to reach a fair, market-based agreement to keep your access to local programming.

Q: I have heard Comcast say that they want to carry local stations, but programmers simply won't let them carry the station anymore. Is that true?

A: No. This is a misleading statement. We are committed to reaching fair, market-based agreements with all the video service providers in our area. We've successfully reached hundreds of multi-year deals with distributors across the country over the past several years, including other major cable companies like Verizon, Spectrum and others. Unfortunately, Comcast has refused to reach a fair, market-based agreement, denying customers access to local programming.

Q: Why should Comcast have to pay to carry station programming when the signal is free over-the-air?

A: Our signal is free to viewers, not to Comcast. Broadcast stations like ours are a big part of Comcast's value to viewers and all we ask for is fair compensation for that value - after all, we are working harder and investing more in our programming and local communities than ever before.

Q: I have heard Comcast say that programmers are demanding big fee increases, which would lead to higher bills. Is that true?

A: No. That is an example of Comcast misleading its own customers. We have reached deals with hundreds of pay-TV providers - all of which felt they were able to reach a fair deal with us while continuing to offer value to their subscribers.

Q: Isn't it unfair that I lost access to a channel that I'm paying for just because two companies can't reach a deal?

A: We agree - it is unfair. After all, you pay your bill to Comcast every month. You deserve the full set of channels you are paying for and you shouldn't miss one minute of the programming you love - consider switching to one of the many providers that continue to carry our station.
